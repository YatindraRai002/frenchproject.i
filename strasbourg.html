Strasbourg

Strasbourg is the capital city of the Grand Est region in France and is known for its unique blend of French and German culture. Located near the border with Germany, it has a rich history marked by its medieval architecture and vibrant atmosphere. The city is home to the European Parliament and is famous for its Christmas market, one of the oldest and largest in Europe.

Choucroute Garnie

Choucroute Garnie is a traditional Alsatian dish, similar to German sauerkraut, but typically served with a variety of meats. It’s a hearty dish perfect for colder months and symbolizes the strong German-French culinary connection in Alsace.


Ingredients

  • 1. Smoked pork belly or bacon
  • 2. Sauerkraut
  • 3. Potatoes
  • 4. White wine
  • 5. Onions and garlic

METHOD OF PREPERATION

STEP 1

Rinse the sauerkraut under cold water to reduce its acidity, then drain.

STEP 2

Sauté chopped onions and garlic in a large pot with a little oil or butter add the sauerkraut, juniper berries, bay leaves, and cloves.

STEP 3

Pour white wine over the sauerkraut and let it simmer for about 30 minutes.

STEP 4

: Add the various meats (sausages, smoked pork belly, ham hock) on top of the sauerkraut. Cover the pot and let it simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours.

STEP 5

About 30 minutes before serving, add peeled potatoes to the pot for a complete meal.

STEP 6

Serve hot with a side of Dijon mustard or crusty bread.
