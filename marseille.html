Marseille City

Marseille, located in the Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur region, is France’s oldest city and second-largest urban center. Known as a bustling Mediterranean port, it has a rich history influenced by Greek, Roman, and North African cultures. The city is celebrated for its coastal charm, historic architecture, vibrant neighborhoods, and diverse food scene.

Bouillabaisse


Bouillabaisse is Marseille's most iconic dish, a hearty fish stew traditionally enjoyed by local fishermen. Originally, it was made with less marketable fish, but today it features various Mediterranean fish and seafood in a flavorful broth infused with herbs and spices.


Ingredients

For the Bouillabaisse
  • 1. Rockfish (like rascasse)
  • 2. Red mullet
  • 3. Monkfish
  • 4. Conger eel
  • 5. Shellfish(mussels, crabs).
  • 6. Tomatoes
  • 7. Leeks
  • 8. Onions
  • 9. Celery
  • 10. Garlic
  • 11. Fennel
  • 12. Thyme
  • 13. Saffron
  • 14. Olive oilr
  • 15. Fish stock
  • 16. Rouille

METHOD OF PREPARATION

STEP 1

Clean and prepare the fish, reserving the heads and bones for the stock.

STEP 2

In a large pot, heat olive oil and sauté onions, leeks, celery, and garlic until softened

STEP 3

Add tomatoes, herbs, and saffron, and pour in the fish stock and white wine. Bring it to a boil.

STEP 4

Add fish and cook each type according to its thickness. Add shellfish last.

STEP 5

Raise the heat under the pan and stir in the beer and add enough beef stock to cover the beef in the pan. Bring to a boil, then cover the pan, reduce the heat to low, and simmer for 1 1/2 hours or until the beef is tender. Keep checking to make sure the beef is simmering and not boiling dry. If it seems a little dry, add a touch more stock. The liquid in the pan also should be thickening slightly.

STEP 6

Serve the broth first, followed by the fish and seafood on a platter, accompanied by rouille and toasted bread.
